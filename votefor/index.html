Myself

I am Parvez Mahbub, a Master’s student at the Department of Computer Science. Along with the study, I am employed as a teaching assistant in different graduate courses, working as a research scholar at Raise Lab and a research intern at Metabob under the Mitacs Accelerate Program. My primary research interest lies in natural language processing, deep learning, and software engineering. Before joining the Master’s program, I worked as a professional software engineer for more than two years. During the job, along with the apparent industry works, I hosted several workshops on different cutting-edge technologies to enhance the calibre of fellow engineers. Apart from academics, I am always active in different extra-curricular activities. During my undergraduate studies, I acted as the IT secretary of CLUSTER — the computer club of Khulna University, Bangladesh, where my primary responsibility was to oversee the technical aspects of different services provided by the Computer Science and Engineering discipline. I also acted as the Head of Promotion of Podium, Khulna University — an organization for improving public speaking skills. My primary responsibility was to host the public speaking sessions and communicate with the guest speakers. While working in these roles, I had to synchronize and maintain a collaborative relationship with several other intra-university and inter-university organizations and technology vendors. Such responsibility helped me nourish the skills to achieve the best mutual benefit for different organizations. Therefore, I believe the Vice President of External Advocacy is the best place for me to contribute for the graduate students of Dalhousie University.

My Platform Points

  1. I will raise concerns with other organizations regarding the increment of tuition fees. I will especially try to minimize the difference in tuition fees for international students.
  2. I will actively work with the Faculty of Graduate Studies (FGS) to make sure the academic expectation of the graduate students is a sweet point between stress and productivity.
  3. As in the post covid era, the delivery and evaluation of different courses are significantly different; I will work with the Faculty of Graduate Studies (FGC) and Dalhousie Student Union (DSU) to ensure the transition is smooth as possible for the students.
  4. I will improve communication and collaboration with the industry and make graduate students ready for the changing need of the neo-normal industry with workshops, seminars, and webinars on latest technological advancement, portfolio building, and representing themselves.
  5. I will try to use my industry experience and connections to incorporate industry evaluation in competitions like 3-minute theses so that graduate students are well aware of industry expectations and standards.

Why Me?

Being a graduate student myself, I am well aware of graduate students’ problems and difficulties. I started my study in the middle of the covid situation and experienced different study models, including online synchro-nous/asynchronous, hybrid, and in-person. I have faced and seen students facing difficulties coping with the different models and still being productive. Therefore, I can work with the Faculty of graduate studies to smooth the transition. Especially when everything is going in-person gradually, complying with the changing industry need would be a critical concern for graduate students in the upcoming days. I believe my industry experience can play a vital role in ensuring external resources to help the students prepare for the changing needs. Furthermore, my former experiences of representing my organization and working with other organizations for mutual benefit will help me to stand out as a Vice President of External Advocacy.

Top Three Things I Will Try To Achieve

Improve communication and collaboration with the industry

Using my industry experience and relations, I will try to diversify the co-op, internship, summer project, and part-time work opportunities. Furthermore, I will try to incorporate industry evaluation in competitions like 3-minute theses to meet the students with industry expectations and standards.

Ensure a smooth transition from online curriculum to offline

While we expect everything to go normal gradually in the upcoming days, it is a matter of being used to the neo-normal life. This is especially true for academic life. Given my experience as a graduate student and a teaching assistant, I will ensure that students face fewer difficulties during the transition.

Preparing students for changing job market

As the covid situation is getting better, more and more industries are going entirely in-person. However, they still want to leverage the usefulness of online tools at the same time. Therefore, I will employ external resources to help students improve their technical competence and prepare for this changing need in the job market.
